The Horizontal

The second aspect of the VT-shaped student is the horizontal, which translates into the interdisciplinary interests and experiences of a person with in-depth knowledge of their primary field. In order to become a more well-rounded individual and a desirable career professional, it is important to receive liberal education directly alongside technical or practical coursework.

For me, this involved completing the requirements for two minors, English Literature and Sociology, while also working on my major. So in addition to having the foundational math and science classes needed to complete a degree in psychology, I also have extensive coursework in social sciences and liberal arts. Currently, I am working to turn that Literature minor into a Literature and Language Bachelor of Arts degree with an option in Literature. Doing this will allow me to further my transdisciplinary knowledge, improve upon my existing skills as a critical thinker and writer, and also build up my basic technological skills, particularly with computers and the efficient use of various authoring and web development programs often used by writers of all fields.
