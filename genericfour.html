The left half of the V represents the informal learning opportunities that a VT-shaped student will benefit from, whether they be student-led organizations or interests groups on campus, or off campus study abroad experiences.

In the three and a half years it took me to get my first degree at Virginia Tech I mostly engaged with the community outside of the classroom through various extracurricular activities. Specifically I was involved in the student-run Delta Psi Nu Local Leadership Fraternity, a co-ed group of students from various majors who have a collective mission to utilize and develop the personal leaderships skills of their members for the purpose of actively engaging with both the VT campus and the local community through service and fundraising activities. During the two years I was involved with the fraternity, we participated in the Big Event, Virginia Tech Relay for Life, on-campus cleaning drives and other volunteer opportunities led by the fraternity members themselves. In addition, we would regularly interact with the larger community through volunteer experiences at Warm Hearth Village, the Lyric Theater, pet adoption drives, and various fundraising events for our membersâ€™ favorite local causes.

The learning opportunities you choose to engage with will likely come to represent much about your time at Virginia Tech. An important part of developing the first half of the V is determining what your passions are, and what you want to accomplish in life most! Where you put your time and energy now will ultimately help guide where your talents take you in the future.
