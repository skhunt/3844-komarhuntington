More About Me

I finished a Bachelor of Science degree in Psychology, and now I'm going back to school to get another bachelors degree – this time in English Literature and Language with an option in Literature. I recently got married, and some new financial opportunities have allowed my husband and I both to return to school this year, getting us miles closer to achieving our goals!

While I’m getting the English degree I have always wanted, I am going to be taking advantage of opportunities that I did not get to, or did not seek out, in the past. It has been my dream to become an academic writer in some regard, so the technological focus that the College of Liberal Arts and Human Sciences places in their coursework will be an essential tool in developing my digital writing skills as an emerging professional.

I’m also going to be working on myself as a VT-shaped individual particularly in the “V” areas where I can most improve! You may have heard about this concept at orientation or Hokie Camp. A VT-shaped student or professional is the essence of what we want our Hokies to strive for, so that they can be as successful as possible in today’s constantly changing world. If I am successful during my time at VT, I will graduate with both the depth and diversity of knowledge, as well as the practical, hands-on experience that I'll need to launch my career after college. If I can do it, so can you!
