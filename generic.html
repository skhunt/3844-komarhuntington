More About Me

I finished a Bachelor of Science degree in Psychology, and now I'm going back to school to get another bachelors degree – this time in English Literature and Language with an option in Literature. I recently got married, and some new financial opportunities have allowed my husband and I both to return to school this year, getting us miles closer to achieving our goals!

While I’m getting the English degree I have always wanted, I am going to be taking advantage of opportunities that I did not get to in the past. It has been my dream to become an academic writer, so the technological focus that the College of Liberal Arts and Human Sciences places in their coursework will be an essential tool in developing my digital writing skills as an emerging professional. I’m also going to be placing a lot of focus on developing myself as a VT-shaped individual, particularly in the V areas where I can most improve!

You may have heard about this concept at orientation or Hokie Camp. A VT-shaped student or professional is the essence of what we want our Hokies to strive for, so that they can be as successful as possible in today’s constantly changing world. These students combine four specific elements of their education and professional development to form their shape, with each element being guided by their personal goals and interests. Specifically, the T represents 1) a student’s depth of knowledge in their field of study as well as 2) the breadth of knowledge that the student possesses over multiple different, possibly related disciplines. At Virginia Tech, we further incorporate aspects of the V-shaped person into this model, by encouraging a dual focus on 3) community engagement, and 4) institutional learning experiences.

By keeping these specific aspects of the model in mind during their time at Virginia Tech, a Tech student can and will graduate with both the depth and diversity of knowledge, as well as the practical, hands-on experience that will be needed to launch successful careers after college.
