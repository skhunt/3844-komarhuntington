My Plans and Recommendations

So what am I going to do with all of these experiences, skills, and knowledge? While working on my English Literature degree, I am also going to be pursuing a new minor in International Relations because I want to improve upon my humanities foundations. Ultimately, once I finish this new degree program, my goal is to apply and be accepted into a masters degree program, preferably in International Studies. I want to take the attitude of service and community learning that I have learned while at Virginia Tech into the rest of my life and career by working in a service industry that can possibly affect worldwide change.

You can chase your dreams and prepare yourself just as fully by striving to attain the VT shape in your own academic and professional life! Just remember some key points to help guide you on your journey at Virginia Tech:

  1. Immerse yourself in your major and become active in your department.
  2. Prioritize your general education classes, and make an effort to take liberal arts electives or select a minor outside of your college.
  3. Take advantage of community engagement or learning opportunities, both in and out of class.
  4. Pick an academic or professional development activity every school year and commit to it (i.e. internships, undergraduate research, study abroad, etc.)

By keeping these key elements of the VT-shaped individual in mind throughout your years at Virginia Tech, you should have no trouble finding your path forward and developing your own tools for success as a future graduate. Good luck!
