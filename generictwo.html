The Vertical

The Vertical component of the VT-shaped student or professional requires that an individual fully immerse themselves in their primary field of study. In addition, they should make an effort to identify their department's areas focus and strength in their curriculum. You'll notice this as you take multiple classes within the same department and see that some aspects of the coursework are repeatedly taught, or emphasized at every stage of learning.

For example, I completed my degree in Psychology from the College of Science, where the departmental focus is largely on methodological practices and research involvement. For every specific niche or subject studied within the discipline of psychology, there is a strong emphasis on the specific methods of study and analysis of that subject within the coursework here at Virginia Tech. I focused in particular on developmental, social, and business psychology classes, and took electives on abnormal psychology, all of which I found endlessly interesting. As a result of receiving my degree and developing my skills in the department's areas of strength (methodology and research publishing), I feel more than adequately prepared to take on a job or project relating to psychological topics and/or research questions, even now, more than two years after graduating.

Whatever your discipline of expertise may be, make an effort to identify the marketable skills and specific types of knowledge that you have. This will help you narrow down your strengths and interests in the future!
